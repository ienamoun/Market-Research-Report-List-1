<p><p>The organic coir market comprises two primary types: white fiber and brown fiber. White fiber, derived from the husk of coconuts, is known for its strength and is often used in products like ropes and mats. In contrast, brown fiber, which comes from the same source but is less processed, is coarser and primarily utilized for applications such as coir pith and erosion control. Both types cater to diverse industries, including horticulture, textiles, and home decor, reflecting their growing demand and sustainability focus.</p></p>
