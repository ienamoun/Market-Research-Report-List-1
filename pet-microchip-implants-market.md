<p><p>Pet microchip implants are categorized based on frequency types: 125 kHz, 128 kHz, and 134.2 kHz. Each frequency offers varying communication range and compatibility with scanners. The 125 kHz microchip is often used for basic identification, while the 128 kHz version is employed for specific regional regulations. The 134.2 kHz microchip, widely regarded as a global standard, is commonly used in pet identification systems, allowing seamless retrieval of pet information across different countries and databases.</p></p>
