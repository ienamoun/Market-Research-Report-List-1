<p><p>Congenital heart diseases (CHD) encompass various types, with the main market segments being hypoplasia, obstructive, and septal defects. Hypoplasia involves underdeveloped heart structures, leading to inadequate blood flow. Obstructive defects hinder normal blood flow due to narrowed or blocked pathways, such as coarctation of the aorta. Septal defects are characterized by abnormal openings between heart chambers, allowing improper blood mixing. Each type requires specific treatments, driving demand for medical devices, surgical interventions, and ongoing patient care in the market.</p></p>
