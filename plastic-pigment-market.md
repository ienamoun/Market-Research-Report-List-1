<p><p>The plastic pigment market is primarily divided into two types: inorganic and organic pigments. Inorganic pigments, derived from mineral sources, offer durability and UV stability, making them ideal for applications in outdoor plastics. Common examples include titanium dioxide and iron oxides. In contrast, organic pigments, synthesized from carbon-based compounds, provide vibrant colors and are favored for their brightness and versatility. They are often used in consumer products like packaging and toys, where aesthetics play a crucial role.</p></p>
