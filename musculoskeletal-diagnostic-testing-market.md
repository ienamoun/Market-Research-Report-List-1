<p><p>The musculoskeletal diagnostic testing market comprises various imaging techniques used to evaluate bone and joint conditions. X-rays provide quick visualization of fractures and abnormalities. Dual-Energy X-Ray Absorptiometry (DXA) measures bone density, assessing osteoporosis risk. Computed Tomography (CT) offers detailed cross-sectional images for complex fractures. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) delivers high-resolution images of soft tissues, including muscles and ligaments. Ultrasonography uses sound waves for real-time imaging of muscles and joints. Other methods may include nuclear medicine or specialized imaging technologies.</p></p>
